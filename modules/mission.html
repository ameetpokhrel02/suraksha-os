Step 1 of 4
The Bait — Fake Ad
It all starts with a tempting social media ad. A "free iPhone," an "investment opportunity," or a "rental deal." You click it — and the chain begins.
💬
Step 2 of 4
The Hook — Building Trust
The scammer contacts you personally. They pretend to know you, compliment you, and build an emotional connection. All of it is manufactured.
🔑
Step 3 of 4
The Steal — OTP Hijack
Now they need access to your accounts. They'll trick you into sharing your verification codes, giving them the keys to your digital life.
🎣
Step 4 of 4
The Kill — Phishing Page
Finally, they send a fake login page that looks identical to Instagram, Facebook, or your bank. One submission and they own everything.

🔓 Why You Were Compromised
📢
Clicked a Fake Ad
Scammers used urgency and social proof (fake likes, comments) to make a scam post look legitimate. Your curiosity brought you into the funnel.
💬
Trusted a Stranger
The scammer used your name, a fake backstory, and emotional manipulation ("You're like family") to bypass your natural skepticism.
🔑
Shared Your OTP
The "I sent it by mistake" trick exploited your helpfulness. OTPs are the keys to your accounts — sharing them is like handing over your house key.
🎣
Entered Credentials on a Fake Page
The phishing page was pixel-perfect. Without checking the URL, you submitted your real password to a server controlled by the attacker.
⚠️ Real-World Consequences
💰
Financial Loss
Scammers drain bank accounts, eSewa wallets, and mobile banking. Average loss in Nepal: Rs. 50,000 – Rs. 5,00,000 per victim.
👤
Identity Theft
Your stolen credentials are used to impersonate you — sending scam messages to your contacts, applying for loans in your name.
🔗
Chain Attacks on Your Contacts
Your hacked account is used to scam your friends and family. They trust messages from "you" — making them easy targets.
📛
Reputation Damage
Compromised social media accounts can post harmful content under your name, damaging personal and professional relationships.
🛡️ Prevention Tips
1️⃣
Never share OTPs. No legitimate service, friend, or support agent will ever ask for your verification code. This is a universal rule.
2️⃣
Check URLs before logging in. Phishing pages look identical but have different domains. Always verify: instagram.com, not instagr4m-login.com.
3️⃣
Enable Two-Factor Authentication. Turn on 2FA for WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram, and your email. Even if passwords leak, 2FA blocks access.
4️⃣
Verify via voice call. If anyone asks for money or codes via text, call them on a normal phone call. Scammers will never agree to a live call.
5️⃣
Report suspicious accounts. Use in-app reporting (WhatsApp, Facebook, Telegram) and contact Nepal Cyber Bureau at 1144 for serious incidents.
